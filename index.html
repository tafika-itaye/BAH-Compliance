Infection Control Policy — v4.2
Clinical · Mandatory for all clinical staff
Awaiting CEO Approval
Policy Document
v4.2 — 14 Mar 2026

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the standards and procedures for infection prevention and control at Blantyre Adventist Hospital, in alignment with COHSASA standards and WHO guidelines.

2. Scope

Applies to all clinical staff, support staff, and visitors. All clinical departments are required to comply with the hand hygiene, PPE, and waste management sections.

3. Key Requirements

3.1 Hand hygiene must be performed at the WHO 5 moments.

3.2 PPE must be worn in all patient contact areas.

3.3 Sharps disposal must follow the colour-coded bin protocol.

3.4 Monthly infection surveillance reports must be submitted to the Quality Officer.

Incident Details
During medication preparation for Ward 3, a dosage of 500mg was drawn up instead of the prescribed 250mg. The error was identified by the supervising nurse before administration. Patient was not affected.
Similar vial labelling between 250mg and 500mg concentration. High workload at time of incident.
Correct dose administered. Incident reported to ward supervisor. Vials segregated and flagged for pharmacy review.
